DEPARTMENT OF YOTUH AFFAIRS

The Ministry of Youth Affairs & Sports was initially set up as the Department of Sports in 1982 at the time of organization of the IX Asian Games in New Delhi . Its name was changed to the Department of Youth affairs & sports during celebration of the International Youth Year, 1985. It became a Ministry on 27th May,2000. Subsequently, the Ministry has been bifurcated in Deptt. of Youth Affairs and Deptt. of Sports under two separate Secretaries w.e.f. 30th April,2008. The specific subjects being dealt with by these two Departments are contained in the Order of the Govt. of India (Allocation of Business) Rules, 1961.

The Youth, i.e. those falling within the age group of 15-29 years constitutes nearly 40% of the total population of India. This group which is the most vibrant and dynamic segment of the country’s population constitutes potentially its most valuable human resource. To optimally tap their constructive and creative energies, the Department pursues the twin objectives of personality building and nation building, i.e. developing the personality of youth and involving them in various nation-building activities. As most of the issues concerning young people are the functions of other Ministries/Departments, like Education, Employment & Training, Health and Family Welfare, etc. the role of the Ministry of Youth Affairs & Sports is to act as a facilitator and catalytic agent.

NATIONAL SERVICE SCHEME

The National Service Scheme (NSS) is a public service program in India, launched in 1969 by the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports. It aims to instill a sense of social responsibility, community service, and leadership skills among students at the high school, college, and university levels. Inspired by Mahatma Gandhi’s ideals of selfless service, NSS encourages young individuals to actively participate in nation-building activities. The program operates through various institutions, engaging students in activities such as rural development, literacy drives, environmental conservation, and health awareness campaigns. Through these initiatives, NSS helps students develop a sense of empathy, teamwork, and civic engagement while addressing real societal challenges.

The NSS motto, "Not Me, But You," emphasizes the importance of putting society before oneself. Volunteers commit to working for two years, engaging in regular activities and special community service camps. These include blood donation drives, Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Clean India Campaign), disaster relief efforts, and initiatives for the welfare of marginalized communities. The program fosters personal growth, discipline, and leadership qualities, preparing students for future responsibilities as socially conscious citizens. By bridging the gap between education and social service, NSS plays a crucial role in promoting national integration, communal harmony, and sustainable development.

NGO PARTNERSHIP SYSTEM

The NGO Partnership System (NGO-PS), also known as NGO-DARPAN, is an initiative by the Government of India to facilitate collaboration between voluntary organizations (VOs) and the government. Managed by NITI Aayog, this system serves as a comprehensive database of NGOs across India, providing them with access to relevant information on various government schemes and grants. By streamlining the interaction between NGOs and different ministries, the system aims to enhance transparency, accountability, and efficiency in fund allocation and project implementation.

Upon registration, NGOs receive a Unique Identification Number (Unique ID), which is necessary for applying for government grants. The platform allows registered NGOs to explore and apply for funding opportunities from different ministries and track the status of their applications. This digital framework not only simplifies administrative procedures but also strengthens the credibility of NGOs by maintaining a verified repository of organizations working in different sectors.

INTERNATIONAL COOPERATION

The Department endeavors to create an international perspective among youth in collaboration with other countries and international agencies/organizations on various youth issues. The Department also collaborates with UN Agencies like United Nations Volunteers (UNV)/ United National Development Programme (UNDP) and the Commonwealth Youth Programme (CYP) on various youth related issues.

Against the backdrop of the National Youth Policy objectives, inter-Alia, to create an International perspective in youth and to involve them in promoting peace and understanding, the International Exchange of Youth Delegations has been conceived as an effective instrument. Under this programme, exchange of Youth Delegations with friendly countries is taken up on reciprocal basis for promoting exchange of ideas, values and culture amongst the youth of different countries and also to develop International understanding.

NEHRU YUVA KENDRA SANGATHAN

Nehru Yuva Kendra Sangathan (NYKS), launched in 1972, is one of the largest youth organisations in the world. NYKS currently has about 8.5 million youth enrolled through 3.01 lakh Youth Clubs/Mahila Mandals. NYKS has presence in 623 Districts through Nehru Yuva Kendras (NYKs). The Objective of the Programme is to develop the personality and leadershipqualities of the youth and to engage them in nation-building activities.

The areas of focus of the NYKS activities include literacy and education, health and family welfare, sanitation and cleanliness, environment conservation, awareness on social issues, women empowerment, rural development, skill development and self-employment, entrepreneurship development, civic education, disaster relief and rehabilitation, etc. The youth associated with Nehru Yuva Kendras are not only socially aware and motivated but are also inclined towards social development work through voluntary efforts.
